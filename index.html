About

Nina Ricci, born Maria Adélaide Nielli in Turin, Italy, in 1883, was a visionary designer who left an indelible mark on the world of fashion. Moving to Paris as a young girl, she embraced her passion for design and quickly rose through the ranks of French haute couture. Nina regarded dress design as a series of problems to be solved. She used her feminine intuition and natural talent to master these skills. Directly working with the fabrics on the mannequin, she sought to discover in the cloth the answer to what the dress would eventually be. In 1932, she founded her eponymous fashion house, where she became known for her refined, feminine style and her ability to capture the essence of timeless elegance. A true innovator, Nina Ricci brought her delicate touch to every creation, combining classic forms with a romantic sensibility. Her legacy endures through the brand she created, which remains a symbol of grace and sophistication to this day.
